## Feedback

Collected in-app via the feedback widget (⭐ rating + note + self-selected role).
**9 responses · average 4.4 / 5.**

**🌾 Farmer**
- ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ — "Being able to draw USDC against my stored wheat without selling early at harvest is exactly what we need. The health factor bar made it clear how much I could safely borrow."
- ⭐⭐⭐⭐ — "Minting the demo receipt and borrowing was smooth. I'd love to see the local currency (TRY) amount next to USDC so I know what I'm actually getting."

**🤝 Cooperative**
- ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ — "Managing our members' receipts could genuinely cut out the middlemen. The warehouse + inspector co-signature (attestation) is what makes it trustworthy for us."
- ⭐⭐⭐⭐ — "Fast and clear. As a co-op we'd want to handle many members' receipts in a single view."

**💧 Liquidity provider**
- ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ — "Supplying USDC and watching the share price grow with interest was satisfying — the vault model is easy to understand. Withdrawing worked instantly too."
- ⭐⭐⭐⭐ — "Good explanation of the yield. Before committing more, I'd like to see historical APR and pool utilization."

**🔍 Just exploring**
- ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ — "Didn't expect a Stellar dApp to feel this polished. Connected Freighter, minted a receipt, borrowed, repaid — the whole loop just worked."
- ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ — "Clean UI and fast transactions. Took me a moment to find the wallet-connect flow at first, but everything after was very smooth."
- ⭐⭐⭐ — "Strong idea and the technical side looks solid. A few buttons felt a little small on mobile, but otherwise the borrow flow was clear."

### What we learned

- **Liked:** the polish, sub-second/low-fee testnet transactions, the clear
  health-factor bar and vault model, and that the full borrow → repay loop just
  works end-to-end.
- **Requested:** local-currency (TRY) amount beside USDC, historical APR / pool
  utilization, a batch view for cooperatives, and larger tap targets on mobile.
- **Shipped in response:** brand-new testnet wallets are now **auto-funded via
  Friendbot** on their first action — previously a first-time user's first
  transaction failed with "Account not found".
